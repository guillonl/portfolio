## üîÑ Process - UX Research Scouting Methodology in 8 Steps

### Step 1: Scoping & Objectives Definition (10-15 min)

**Objective:** Clarify research scope and business objectives.

**Actions:**

**1. Identify desired research type**

Available research types:
- **Competitive Analysis**: Analyze 3-5 direct competitors
- **Best Practices Benchmark**: Identify industry leaders (not necessarily competitors)
- **Pattern Research**: Catalog design patterns for specific use case
- **Compliance Audit**: Verify WCAG, GDPR, ADA compliance
- **Academic Research Synthesis**: Synthesize UX scientific studies
- **Innovation Scouting**: Identify emerging trends and opportunities

**2. Define analysis criteria**

Typical criteria:
- **Usability**: Ease of use, intuitiveness, learnability
- **Accessibility**: WCAG compliance, keyboard nav, screen reader
- **Visual Design**: Aesthetics, consistency, branding
- **Performance**: Load time, responsiveness
- **Features**: Feature richness, innovation
- **Business Model**: Pricing, monetization, value prop

**3. Clarify business context**

Initial questions:
- Which industry? (e-commerce, B2B SaaS, fintech, healthcare, etc.)
- Which flow/feature to analyze? (checkout, onboarding, dashboard, etc.)
- Objective: Design inspiration? Compliance check? Competitive intel?
- Geography: US, EU, Global? (important for compliance)
- Target audience: B2B, B2C, B2B2C?

**4. Define competitors or benchmarks**

**Option A: You provide competitor list**
- Ex: "Analyze Stripe, PayPal, Square (payment flows)"

**Option B: I propose competitors**
- Based on industry and criteria
- Top 3-5 market players
- Mix: Market leaders + innovators + niche players

**Output:**
- Research type defined
- Analysis criteria listed (3-5 priority criteria)
- Business context clarified
- Competitor/benchmark list (3-5 max to remain actionable)

### Step 5: Compliance Audit (WCAG, GDPR, ADA) (20-30 min)

**Objective:** Audit regulatory compliance (accessibility and privacy) of competitors and identify gaps.

**1. WCAG 2.1/2.2 Accessibility Compliance**

Verify conformance with POUR principles:

**P - Perceivable**
- [ ] Images have alt text
- [ ] Videos have captions/transcripts
- [ ] Color contrast ‚â• 4.5:1 (text), ‚â• 3:1 (UI components)
- [ ] Content adaptable (responsive, no info lost at 200% zoom)

**O - Operable**
- [ ] Complete keyboard navigation (no mouse required)
- [ ] Visible focus indicators (outline on tab)
- [ ] No keyboard traps (can tab out)
- [ ] Sufficient time (no auto-timeouts, or user can extend)

**U - Understandable**
- [ ] Language declared (`<html lang="en">`)
- [ ] Clear labels (form fields, buttons)
- [ ] Explicit error messages ("Email format invalid" not "Error")
- [ ] Consistent navigation (menu same position across pages)

**R - Robust**
- [ ] Valid HTML (no broken tags)
- [ ] ARIA labels where needed (custom components)
- [ ] Compatible with assistive technologies (screen readers)

**WCAG Levels:**
- **Level A**: Minimum (minimum legal requirement)
- **Level AA**: Standard (most regulations require AA)
- **Level AAA**: Gold standard (strict, not always achievable)

**3. ADA/Section 508 Compliance (US Accessibility Law)**

**ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act):**
- Requires websites accessible to people with disabilities
- No official standard, but courts use WCAG 2.1 Level AA as reference
- Non-compliance = lawsuits (Domino's Pizza ADA lawsuit 2019)

**Section 508 (US Government):**
- Federal agencies must comply
- Based on WCAG 2.0 Level AA (updating to WCAG 2.1)

**Audit:**
```

‚Üí Legal risk: HIGH (could be lawsuit target)
‚Üí Recommendation: Urgent accessibility remediation
```

European standard aligned with WCAG 2.1 Level AA.

**EU Regulations:**
- **European Accessibility Act (EAA)**: Effective 2025, mandates accessibility for products/services

French standard based on WCAG 2.1.

**Levels:**
- **Non-compliant**: < 50% criteria met
- **Partially compliant**: 50-100% Level AA criteria

## ‚úÖ Best Practices

### DO ‚úÖ

1. **Analyze 3-5 competitors max** (no more, otherwise paralysis)
2. **Focus depth over breadth** (deep dive 2 flows > superficial 10 flows)
3. **Link findings to data** (Baymard stats, NN/g studies, not just opinions)
4. **Include annotated screenshots** (visual > text)
5. **Benchmark best-in-class** (not just direct competitors)
6. **Contextualize recommendations** (what works for Amazon ‚â† works for you)
7. **Prioritize actionability** (Quick Wins > Strategic Bets > Innovation)
8. **Validate compliance** (WCAG, GDPR = legal risk mitigation)
9. **Monitor trends** (quarterly reviews, not one-time analysis)
10. **Document learnings** (build reusable knowledge base)

### DON'T ‚ùå

1. **Don't blindly copy** (context matters, adapt not clone)
2. **Don't ignore legal compliance** (WCAG, GDPR violations = lawsuits)
3. **Don't over-analyze** (10 competitors = paralysis, focus top 3-5)
4. **Don't cherry-pick** (analyze failures too, learn from mistakes)
5. **Don't forget mobile** (60%+ traffic mobile, analyze mobile UX)
6. **Don't neglect performance** (beautiful but slow = bad UX)
7. **Don't assume "industry leader = best UX"** (market share ‚â† UX quality)
8. **Don't ignore accessibility** (15% users have disabilities, huge market)
9. **Don't confuse trends and hype** (validate durability before invest)
10. **Don't do one-time analysis** (competitive landscape evolves, monitor continuously)
